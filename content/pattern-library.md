## Executive Summary

The Evergreen Ad Generator draws from two interlocking layers of methodology. The tactical layer — contributed by Zac's ad templates, Ben Valen's NHB guide, and the Evergreen Framework templates — provides a concrete library of named ad structures, opener formulas, headline swipes, body-copy frameworks, proof constructions, bonus stack patterns, CTAs, and sign-off sequences. These are not abstract principles; they are slot-fillable frameworks proven in live ad campaigns. The doctrine layer — Ben Valen's Belief Engineering Masterclass — provides the philosophical and architectural foundation that explains WHY those tactical elements work: every conversion failure is a Belief Gap, every ad's job is to build a structurally sound Belief Bridge by installing six categories of belief in the correct emotional sequence.

Across all four sources, three structural principles appear universally. First: the opening line (called a "Headturner" in the Zac/Framework sources and the "Ad Opener / Alternative Headline" in the NHB guide) is the single most critical element in any ad — the same core ad with five different headturners produces radically different results, and if the headturner fails, "it's game over." Second: the ad's job is to presell and prime, not to close — by the time a prospect clicks, they should already be sold on the outcome, the mechanism, and the credibility of the person offering it. Third: belief work is not optional decoration — it is the structural engineering underneath every copy decision, from the opener (which must move the prospect from Closed to Receptive State) through the body (which installs the six belief categories) to the CTA (which should feel like a natural next step, not a push).

The voice across all sources shares a distinct signature: casual authority (real results stated plainly, often deliberately understated), empathy-first framing (enter the prospect's problem before naming the solution), structural specificity (named mechanisms, numbered steps, emoji-punctuated bullets), and a warm personal close (sign by name, add a P.S. that handles the lingering objection, P.P.S. with an outrageous guarantee). Emojis are used as functional punctuation — they signal tone, mark transformation moments, direct the eye to links, and replace formal section headers with something more conversational. When generating ads from this library, preserve this voice exactly: do not sanitize the colloquialisms, do not replace emojis with words, do not upgrade casual money references to specific large figures unless user-provided.

## Core Frameworks & Methodologies

### Long-Form Evergreen Ad Framework (Master Structure) [CORE] [domains: ad-frameworks, universal]
- **What it is:** A 10-block sequential structure that every evergreen Facebook ad follows, regardless of whether it leans "direct" or "story." Every named template (Templates 1–5, Story Ads 1–7) is a variation of this master structure with different emphasis or sequencing per block.
- **Steps/Components (in order):**
  1. **Headturner** — Opening hook; first visible line of primary text. Most critical element. "If you miss this, it's game over."
  2. **Story Elements** — Always present, even if just one line. "Anything that sparks a conscious or subconscious image of pain or transformation."
  3. **Problem Story** — Walk the hero through their current active problems.
  4. **End of the Road** — The "life or death" moment. What happens if they don't change?
  5. **Turnaround Story** — Hero takes the heroic path; mechanism/solution is introduced.
  6. **Offer Intro (CTA #1)** — Introduce the offer with a link inside the primary text.
  7. **What Is It** — Brief summary of exactly what the offer is and how it works.
  8. **Bonuses** — Named bonus stack; personal favorite called out with reason.
  9. **Proof** — Claims backed with data: testimonials, client counts, or specific results.
  10. **CTA (Sign-Off)** — Name sign-off + P.S. counter-objection + P.P.S. guarantee. "A choose your own adventure on how you want to end the ad."
- **When to use it:** As the architectural checklist for every ad created. Direct Ads compress or skip blocks 2–5. Story Ads expand them.
- **Key quotes:** "If you miss this, it's game over." / "Even if it's just one line. It could be one line: 'Move away from X to Y'" / "Most people buy for the bonuses."
- **Sources:** framework-and-templates-extracted.md (primary), zac1-extracted.md, NHB-ads-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** All six — Problem (blocks 3–4), Solution/Outcome (block 5), Product (blocks 6–8), Credibility (block 9), Identity/Self (blocks 2–3), Outcome (block 1)

### Two-Stage Prime & Build Framework [CORE] [domains: ad-frameworks, universal]
- **What it is:** Every ad has exactly two sequential jobs. Stage 1: stop the scroll and capture curiosity/intrigue (headline + opener + first 3 seconds of video/image). Stage 2: build intent, desire, and install necessary beliefs (rest of body copy + rest of video/images). Ben Valen's framing of this is "Prime them to click, then buy." Zac's equivalent is preselling — "Evergreen Ads that presell strangers BEFORE they hit the page."
- **Steps/Components:**
  - **Stage 1 (Creating Receptivity):** Headline + Ad Opener / Headturner + first 3 seconds of video or first image. Goal: move prospect from Closed State to Receptive State.
  - **Stage 2 (Belief Engineering):** Rest of body copy. Goal: install the six belief categories in sequence.
- **When to use it:** As the master organizing principle for evaluating every element of every ad — ask "is this element doing Stage 1 work or Stage 2 work?"
- **Key quotes:** "Prime them to click, then buy." / "I focus 98% of my attention on creating Evergreen Ads that presell strangers BEFORE they hit the page."
- **Sources:** NHB-ads-extracted.md, belief-engineering-extracted.md, zac1-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** Stage 1 — Outcome Beliefs, Problem Beliefs (to create receptivity); Stage 2 — all six in sequence

### The Six Categories of Beliefs (The Six Bricks) [SINGLE] [domains: belief-engineering, universal]
- **What it is:** A classification system of the six fundamental belief types every buyer must hold before they will confidently convert. Each maps to a specific buyer question that the ad must answer.
- **Steps/Components (in order):**

  | # | Belief Category | Core Buyer Question | Must Install |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 1 | Outcome Beliefs | "Is the outcome real, attainable, and worth it?" | Possibility + Attainability + Desirability |
  | 2 | Identity/Self Beliefs | "Is this possible for ME? Am I capable/worthy?" | "People like me can succeed." |
  | 3 | Problem Beliefs | "Is this problem real, serious, urgent?" | Must buy YOUR diagnosis of the problem |
  | 4 | Solution Beliefs | "Is this the right approach?" | Mechanism must feel valid, smart, emotionally safe |
  | 5 | Product Beliefs | "Is THIS specific offer the best embodiment?" | Clearest, fastest, safest path to the solution |
  | 6 | Credibility Beliefs | "Can I trust this person/company?" | You know what you're doing + will deliver + crossing is safer than staying |

- **Critical rule:** Product Beliefs (features/benefits) are Brick 5 of 6. Most marketers skip to Brick 5 immediately — this leaves the bridge structurally incomplete.
- **Key quotes:** "There are six fundamental categories of beliefs that must exist --- no exceptions --- for a buyer to confidently cross the bridge." / "People buy when they have certain beliefs. If they don't have those beliefs, they won't buy, no matter how good the offer is."
- **Sources:** belief-engineering-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** All six — this IS the classification

### Belief Transition States (Closed → Receptive → Transformed) [SINGLE] [domains: belief-engineering, universal]
- **What it is:** A three-state model of the internal psychological modes buyers move through. Belief installation is only possible in Receptive State.
- **Steps/Components:**
  - **Closed State:** Default guarded state. Resistant to new ideas. Protecting current worldview. Cannot install beliefs here. Must first disarm, create emotional safety, invite curiosity.
  - **Receptive State:** Open, willing to entertain new ideas, exploring possibilities. This is where Belief Engineering becomes possible.
  - **Transformed State:** All necessary beliefs in place. Internal coherence achieved. Buyer is naturally pulled toward action with little or no pressure. No forcing needed.
- **Key principle:** "You cannot install beliefs inside Closed State. You must first shift emotional state — then engineer beliefs." / "Movement isn't logical — it's emotional first."
- **When to use it:** The headline, opener, and first 3 seconds of video are dedicated to moving from Closed → Receptive. Everything after is Belief Engineering toward Transformed.
- **Sources:** belief-engineering-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** Outcome Beliefs and Identity Beliefs are the primary levers for creating the Closed-to-Receptive shift

### Direct Ad Framework [CORE] [domains: ad-frameworks, micro-offer]
- **What it is:** A compressed ad structure that states the offer, result, and price directly — no long story buildup required. Extremely effective for sub-$27 offers. Compresses the Evergreen Ad master structure by collapsing the problem/story blocks and leading with the offer or result.
- **Sub-types (from Zac's system):**
  - **Direct Ad #1 — Speed-to-Result:** Headturner (price + tool + speed) → 3 Instant Results bullets ("Turn [X] into [Y]") → Proof line → CTA
  - **Direct Ad #2 — Do This, Get That (Ultra-Short):** One punchy action line → proof → 👉link only
  - **Direct Ad #3 — Deadline/Urgency:** Hard deadline baked in; works for live workshops + holiday specials
  - **Direct Ad #4 — Step-By-Step Process:** "If I wanted to [outcome] before [common action], this is what I would do:" → 5 numbered emoji steps → discovery story → bonus stack → case study → CTA
  - **Direct Ad #5 — Daily Habit/Insight Email System:** "Here's how I [outcome] in just [time]..." → 5 steps → Insight 💡 reveal → authority line → offer → proof → PS guarantee
- **When to use it:** Start here for all sub-$27 offers. When the result can be stated in one sentence. When the offer mechanism is simple and demonstrable.
- **Key quotes:** "They work so damn good. One client I wrote these types of ads for is crushing it with the exact same ads one year later. While adding over 1000 buyers to his list at a nice juicy profit (not bad)." / "Start with these if you're selling something under $27."
- **Sources:** zac1-extracted.md, framework-and-templates-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** Outcome (speed-to-result), Product (direct offer statement), Credibility (proof line), Problem (Instant Results bullet contrast)

### Story-Driven Ad Framework [CORE] [domains: ad-frameworks, story-driven]
- **What it is:** Longer-form ads that open with an emotional hook or narrative, build a problem/solution story arc, then present the offer. Go-to for mid-ticket ($50+) offers or when the landing page is thin and the ad must do heavy lifting.
- **Sub-types (from Zac's system):**
  - **Story Ad #1 — Short-Form Emotional (Parent/Relationship Hook):** Emotional quote from key relationship → validation statement → pain bullets → root cause → rejection of conventional solutions → offer
  - **Story Ad #2 — Long-Form "Last Resort":** Client desperation quote → problem story (tried everything) → "haunting" line → unique mechanism metaphor → offer → proof → sign-off + guarantee
  - **Story Ad #3 — "Missing Ingredient" Presell:** "There's a subtle yet mission critical ingredient you must have..." → one thing missing → why it works → authority → offer
  - **Story Ad #4 — Business Result / Evergreen Ads Positioning:** Impressive result claim → methodology reveal → "fire your agency" pattern interrupt → hands-free method story → workshop offer
  - **Story Ad #5 — "Stuck in Neutral" Subconscious Belief:** Client dialogue → problem story → "stuck in neutral" metaphor → root cause (subconscious belief) → solution → P.S. counter-objection → P.P.S. guarantee
  - **Story Ad #6 — Business Model Shift / Pattern Interrupt:** "In [year], I shut down my [old model]..." → results → solution → free value → warning pattern interrupt → vision + CTA
- **When to use it:** Mid-ticket ($50+) offers. When buyers are skeptical or have "tried everything." When the landing page needs the ad to do the heavy lifting.
- **Key quotes:** "These are typically longer from Evergreen Ads. They're my go to when I'm selling Mid Ticket Offers Priced Over $50 or don't have a lot of copy on the landing page so I want to let the ads do the heavy lifting."
- **Sources:** zac1-extracted.md, framework-and-templates-extracted.md, NHB-ads-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** Problem (problem story), Identity/Self (validation + "not alone"), Solution (mechanism reveal), Credibility (authority + proof), Outcome (transformation bullets), Product (offer details)

### Named Ad Templates — NHB System [RECURRING] [domains: ad-frameworks]
- **What it is:** Ben Valen's 7 named structural ad templates, each designed for a different emotional entry point.
- **Sub-types:**
  - **Ad 1 — Belief Shifter / Misconception / Root Cause:** Opens by naming the market's biggest false belief → destroys it → names root cause → personal origin story → mechanism → CTA. Best for: markets stuck on wrong solutions (medication, generic diets, wrong exercises).
  - **Ad 2 — Why What How:** Student/prospect quote voicing the problem → names false market belief → refutes it → explains what IS true → mechanism → testimonials → urgency close. Best for: correcting wrong market-held information.
  - **Ad 3 — Hate / Story:** Opens in scene with a character who hates their reality → transformation within the lesson/session → "If you want..." aspiration stack → CTA. Best for: shame, self-doubt, identity-level pain (voice, body image, relationships).
  - **Ad 4 — Why / Root Cause / Outcome:** "Why are people struggling to [goal]?" → everything tried and failed → root cause → counter-intuitive insight → unique mechanism → proof → CTA. Best for: prospects who've tried the full gamut of mainstream solutions.
  - **Ad 5 — Internal Dialogue / Story:** Written in first person as the prospect's emotional story → shame spiral → rock-bottom → tried everything → resolution → mechanism → empowerment close → "I will guide you. With care and gentleness. And without judgment." Best for: identity-level shame (weight, appearance, relationships).
  - **Ad 6 — Why / What's Wrong With Me / Root Cause:** Opens with self-blaming inner monologue → validates → "most diet plans are too one size fits all" → reframe of failure → personalized solution → warm CTA. Best for: self-blame prospects.
  - **Ad 7 — Remove the Retreat:** Body copy ingredient/template that removes the "do nothing" option as viable. Full skeleton incomplete in source — listed as named template. Best for: mid-to-lower body copy after empathy and root cause established.
- **Key quotes:** "What's the biggest misconception about ADHD in kids? That medication is the only way to treat it..." / "It isn't that you failed. It's because the plan was made for someone else. It wasn't made for you." / "I will guide you. With care and gentleness. And without judgment."
- **Sources:** NHB-ads-extracted.md, belief-engineering-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** Ad 1 → Problem + Solution; Ad 2 → Problem + Solution + Identity; Ad 3 → Identity + Outcome; Ad 4 → Problem + Solution + Credibility; Ad 5 → Identity + Problem + Outcome; Ad 6 → Identity + Problem; Ad 7 → Problem (urgency/inaction)

### The Belief Engineering Ads Process (Three Phases) [SINGLE] [domains: belief-engineering, universal]
- **What it is:** A three-phase operational workflow for building any ad from the Belief Bridge perspective.
- **Steps/Components:**
  - **Phase 1 — Raw Materials (Research):** Who is the target audience? Why don't they currently have the outcome? What Blocking Beliefs do they hold? What Necessary Beliefs must they hold? What are their perceived problems? Can the Fatal Flaw be used? How does the product work? What don't they want to have/be/do to get the outcome?
  - **Phase 2 — Constructing the Belief Bridge (Architecture):** Creating Receptivity (headline + opener + first 3 seconds) → Belief Engineering (dissolving Blocking Beliefs + installing Necessary Beliefs in order: Outcome → Problem → Solution → Product → Credibility → Identity)
  - **Phase 3 — Execution Decisions:** What are we selling? What is the #1 problem and #1 outcome? What's the unique mechanism (Hidden Lock / Master Key / Fatal Flaw)? Decide on opener type, body type, CTA type.
- **Key quotes:** "The purpose of marketing, copywriting, and advertising is to dissolve the Blocking Beliefs that prevent the sale and install the Necessary Beliefs that facilitate the sale."
- **Sources:** belief-engineering-extracted.md
- **Activates (Belief Categories):** All six — this IS the production workflow

## Belief System & Principles

### The Headturner Is the Whole Game [CORE]
- **Statement:** The opening line of any ad is the single most critical element. The same core ad with five different headturners will produce RADICALLY different results. If this fails, nothing else matters.
- **Why they hold this:** Tested in live campaigns. Allows efficient A/B testing of pain points without rewriting full ads.

### Preselling Happens in the Ad, Not on the Page [CORE]
- **Statement:** The ad's job is to presell the prospect so thoroughly that by the time they hit the landing page, they are already sold. The landing page closes; the ad presells.
- **Why they hold this:** Directly tied to Evergreen Ads methodology — the creative-first approach. A well-presold buyer converts regardless of landing page quality.
- **Key quote:** "Evergreen Ads that presell strangers BEFORE they hit the page." / "Notice how someone is going to have a VERY clear idea what's happening when they hit the page."

### The Belief Gap Is the Root Cause of Every Non-Conversion [SINGLE]

- **Key quote:** "At the core of every non-conversion -- whether it's a cold prospect ignoring your ad, a warm lead ghosting your funnel, or a buyer hesitating at the final checkout page -- lies a single root cause: A Belief Gap."

## Tactical Playbook

### Headturner / Opener Tactics [domains: openers, headlines]

- **Test 5 headturners on the same core ad** [CORE] — Same ad body, 5 different headturners = radically different results without writing new ads. Most efficient optimization move available.
- **Three things the best headturners do** [CORE] — (1) Pull the reader into their problem state immediately, (2) make an offer and future-pace the result, (3) demonstrate an easy path forward. Must do at least one of these.
- **Go-to headturner types to test (priority order)** [CORE] — 1. Why PROBLEM opener, 2. HOW TO OUTCOME opener, 3. "What's the biggest misconception" opener.
- **Source dialogue lines from real audiences** [CORE] — "Pull these from Facebook groups, application forms, reddit, book reviews...great tool to use." Use these verbatim as Story Ad headturners.
- **Opener creates Receptive State, not belief installation** [SINGLE] — The opener's only job is to shift state from Closed to Receptive. It is not yet doing belief work.

---

### Headline Tactics [domains: headlines]

- **Start every headline with a verb** [RECURRING] — "Verbs lead to action and you are getting a person to take an action. By default, start with a verb."
- **Outcome must be crystal clear from headline alone** [RECURRING] — "The outcome of your offer should be crystal clear from just the headline."
- **Dan Kennedy Classified Ad Test** [SINGLE] — Strip your ad to just the headline and a phone number. If people would not respond in a newspaper classifieds section, the headline is failing.
- **Add "without [objection]" to the headline** [RECURRING] — Neutralizes the #1 resistance before the body copy has to address it. "Relieve back pain & sciatica | no surgery."
- **Add "the thing" at the end of the headline** [SINGLE] — Specificity extension: "Hollywood's 'Fitness Secret' For Men" → "Hollywood's 'Men's Fitness Secret' | 6wk Program."

---

### Body Copy Tactics [domains: body-copy]

- **Chunk long story ads into named sections** [SINGLE] — "It's very long but notice how it's chunked into sections below." Chunking prevents overwhelm and preserves readability.
- **Don't get technical with step-by-step copy** [SINGLE] — "Don't get too technical with the steps - that's where most people screw up. Instead, you simply want to show the big picture overview." Keep the 5-step framework at the bird's-eye view level only.
- **Use 3 or 5 false solutions (not 4)** [SINGLE] — Template 4 explicitly: "OPTIONAL - use 3 or 5 false solutions" — odd numbers are the standard.
- **Always include a "life or death" moment** [RECURRING] — "We want to always have a 'life or death' moment where the character has to face a choice. Either accept the current outcome or pivot to a new reality."
- **Root cause before mechanism** [RECURRING] — Name what's wrong with mainstream solutions before introducing the new mechanism. The "mask the symptoms" logic is the bridge.
- **"It's Not Your Fault" removes self-blame** [SINGLE] — A specific belief-dissolving technique targeting Identity/Self Beliefs. Removes shame that sustains Blocking Beliefs.
- **Use a P.S. counter-objection for skeptical markets** [CORE] — In transformation/healing/personal development niches, add a P.S. that directly acknowledges "if you've tried this before and it didn't work" — turns the most common objection into a reason to trust you more.
- **"If you want… If you want… Then [CTA]" stack** [SINGLE] — Desire-amplification technique. Each line casts a different net, increasing the probability that at least one resonates.

---

### Proof & Social Proof Tactics [domains: proof]

- **Proof formula** [CORE] — "Responsible for helping over [Number] [Client Type] like [Notable Examples] achieve [Specific Result] without [What They Want to Avoid]." Always include what they avoid, not just what they get.
- **Always back claims with proof inline** [CORE] — "I always like to make claims and back them up with proof. So adding a proof element 'After working with ### of clients, I haven't seen anything work better.'"
- **Named, located, quantified testimonials** [RECURRING] — Include name, age, location, and specific measurable result. Also address skepticism objection directly in the testimonial when possible.
- **"Are you next?" after a testimonial** [SINGLE] — Three words that turn a proof point into a personal challenge.

---

### Bonus Stack Tactics [domains: bonuses]

- **"The bonuses are just plain silly..."** [RECURRING] — Self-aware hyperbole that creates a smile before the list; signals generosity.
- **Call out a personal favorite bonus with a reason** [RECURRING] — "My favorite is [BONUS] because [RESULT IT GETS]" — personalizes the offer.
- **Bonuses are a primary conversion driver** [RECURRING] — Not an afterthought. Structured like a mini-offer: name, benefit, specific result.

---

### CTA & Emoji Tactics [domains: cta, voice]

- **Always put an arrow or finger before every link** [CORE] — "Notice how I ALWAYS put an arrow or finger pointing to the link." Use 👉🏼 or 👉🏻 consistently. Non-negotiable.
- **Place 👇 before the final CTA link** [CORE] — Consistent directional emoji pointing down to the link on the line below.
- **Multiple links = more clicks** [RECURRING] — "Multiple links in an ad is great as people will click at different points." Include a link inside primary text, not just at the end.
- **Use emoji bullets for Instant Results sections** [RECURRING] — One emoji per line, 3 lines maximum (Direct Ads). Emojis signal transformation type (pleasure, excitement, growth).
- **Use numbered emoji steps** [RECURRING] — 1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣ 4️⃣ 5️⃣ for step-by-step process sections; visual and scannable.

### Guarantee Tactics [domains: cta, body-copy]

- **Outrageous guarantee framing** [CORE] — Frame it as something that makes your accountant have "a mini heart attack" — signals confidence and generosity simultaneously.
- **Full refund up to a YEAR + eat the fees** [RECURRING] — "I'll give you a full refund up to a YEAR later, no questions asked. And I'll even eat the payment processing fees for you." The "eat the fees" line adds specificity that signals genuine sincerity.
- **Integrate the guarantee inline** [RECURRING] — "End procrastination and get more done tomorrow than you did all last week or you don't pay." Not just in the P.S.

### Structural Meta-Tactics [domains: universal]

- **Fill all five description fields for Meta Flexible Ad Formats** [SINGLE] — Because Meta may swap descriptions into any position — headline, primary text, etc. — all five must work as standalone units.
- **Tweak the opening of an existing ad before rebuilding** [SINGLE] — "This is actually a tweaked version of Direct Ad #5. I changed the opening but kept the offer all the same." — Change the angle of entry, not the whole structure.
- **Message match: ad headline and landing page headline must align** [SINGLE] — "Notice how someone is going to have a VERY clear idea what's happening when they hit the page." — Alignment = higher conversion.
- **Discovery story adds earned authority** [SINGLE] — Show where the method came from (a mentor, specific experience, modified formula). Borrowed credibility from a source is legitimate.

## Definitions & Distinctions Dictionary

| Term | Their Definition | Standard Definition / How It Differs |
|---|---|---|
| Evergreen Ad | A creative-first ad that presells strangers before they hit the landing page and runs profitably for months or years without being touched or refreshed | Common usage = "timeless content"; here = specifically built to run indefinitely without account optimization or creative rotation |
| Headturner | The opening line or first 1–3 sentences of an ad — its job is to stop the scroll and pull the reader in; the same ad with 5 different headturners gets radically different results | Common usage = "headline" only; here includes the first line of body copy as a distinct named element with its own singular job |
| Ad Opener / "Alternative Headline" | The first line(s) of ad body copy that function as a second headline — sole job is scroll-stopping and curiosity capture, not belief installation | Usually called "the hook" or "the first line" — here it is a distinct named structural element with a specific singular function |
| Direct Ad | A short ad format that states the offer, result, and price directly — no long story, no extended buildup | Common usage = any ad that sells directly; here it's a specific sub-genre with defined structural elements (Headturner → Instant Results → Proof → CTA) |
| Story Ad | A longer-form ad that opens with an emotional hook or narrative, builds a problem/solution arc, then presents the offer | Not synonymous with "long copy ad" — has specific story architecture including life-or-death moment, turnaround, and offer reveal with proof |
| Belief Gap | "The psychological and emotional distance between where your prospect's current beliefs are and the beliefs they would need to hold to confidently and naturally say yes." | Common usage = "awareness gap" or "desire gap" — here the obstacle is located in belief (emotional/psychological), not knowledge or motivation |
| Belief Bridge | The structure that moves a prospect from their current state to a buying decision — must be structurally sound, emotionally safe, cognitively coherent | Usually unnamed — conflated with "the funnel" or "the copy"; here it is an explicitly architectural metaphor with specific load-bearing belief components |
| Belief Bridge Blueprint™ | Ben Valen's proprietary sequence of six belief categories that must be installed in the correct emotional order to create a natural, safe passage to action | Proprietary framework; carries the ™ — signals a codified, teachable, architectural system |
| The Six Bricks | The six fundamental belief categories — Outcome, Identity/Self, Problem, Solution, Product, Credibility — that must all be in place for natural conversion | Standard copy talks about "AIDA" or "features/benefits" — here the six bricks are structural, sequential, and load-bearing; none optional |
| Shaky Bridge | Marketing materials that look fine on the surface but leave the prospect's brain unconvinced, uncertain, or emotionally on edge — missing belief planks | Standard marketing treats underperforming copy as a tactical problem; here it is diagnosed as structural failure |
| Blocking Beliefs | Beliefs the prospect currently holds that prevent the sale — must be dissolved before Necessary Beliefs can be installed | Standard copy calls these "objections" — here they are internal psychological structures that must be dissolved, not just addressed |
| Necessary Beliefs | The specific, nameable belief states a prospect must hold for conversion to happen naturally | Standard copy calls these "buying triggers" or "motivations" — here they are deliberate installation targets, not passive states to appeal to |
| Closed State | Default guarded state. Resistant to new ideas. Protecting current worldview. No belief installation possible. | Often treated as a fixed audience characteristic ("cold traffic") — here it is a manageable state that must be actively shifted |
| Receptive State | Open, willing to entertain new ideas, exploring possibilities. The state where Belief Engineering becomes possible. | Often assumed to already exist when an ad is served — here it must be actively created by the headline/opener |
| Transformed State | All necessary beliefs in place. Internal coherence achieved. Buyer is naturally pulled toward action with little or no pressure. | Often confused with "convinced" — here it describes a complete internal realignment where conversion feels inevitable |
| Fatal Flaw | A narrative technique that repositions the prospect's perceived problem(s) as merely symptoms of a deeper, underlying problem they haven't correctly diagnosed | Standard copy might call this a "villain reveal" or "paradigm shift" — here it is a specific, deployable structural element in the ad body |
| Hidden Lock | The mechanism of the problem — the real underlying cause masked by the symptoms the prospect is aware of | Unique mechanism naming convention; not standard copywriting terminology |
| Master Key | The mechanism of the solution — why this specific approach unlocks what others cannot | Paired with Hidden Lock; together they form the unique mechanism architecture of the ad |
| Micro Offer | A dead-simple, low-cost offer (under $27) designed to add buyers (not freebie seekers) to a list semi-passively, every day | Common usage = any small digital product; here specifically architected as a list-building mechanism that adds pre-sold buyers |
| Freebie Seekers | People who join a list, attend a webinar, or consume content but have no intention or ability to buy | Not a demographic — a behavioral category. The Micro Offer filters them out by requiring payment, however small |
| Presold Leads / Buyers | People who already believe in the seller and their method before they get on a call or see a sales page — generated through insight content | Not leads from a lead magnet or webinar registration — people whose buying objections have been pre-handled through content |
| Insight (Content) | The core ingredient in emails and posts that makes strangers "lean in" — pairing everyday situations with expertise-based perspective | Not "value content" broadly — specifically the act of providing a new lens on the reader's own situation, thoughts, or problems |
| Instant Results | A 3-bullet section using "Turn [Problem] into [Outcome]" format in Direct Ads — demonstrating the speed of transformation | Not a generic "results fast" promise — a specific structural element with a defined format |
| End of the Road | The "life or death" moment in an evergreen story ad — where the character must choose: accept current outcome or pivot | Not a cliffhanger — a consequence-driven fork in the road that forces a decision |
| Turnaround Story | The moment the hero rises up and takes the heroic path forward — introduction of the mechanism | Not a resolution — it's the pivot that bridges problem to offer |
| Bonuses Block | A dedicated section in the ad specifically outlining bonuses, with a personal favorite called out by name and reason | Not an afterthought — treated as a primary conversion driver ("most people buy for the bonuses") |

## Generation Guidelines

### How to Use This Library to Generate Ads

This section is the operational manual for the Evergreen Ad Generator. When generating any ad from user inputs, follow these rules in order.

---

### Rule 1: Select a Framework Based on Inputs

Use this decision tree:

**Offer price under $27 OR offer has a fast, demonstrable result?**
→ Use a **Direct Ad Framework** sub-type.
- Simple mechanism, single result → Direct Ad #2 (ultra-short) or Template 3
- Speed-to-result is the headline story → Direct Ad #1
- Live event or real deadline → Direct Ad #3
- Step-by-step teachable process → Direct Ad #4
- Content/email/presell positioning → Direct Ad #5

**Offer price $50+ OR landing page is thin OR buyer has likely tried everything?**
→ Use a **Story-Driven Ad Framework** sub-type.
- Emotional niche (parenting, relationships, burnout) → Story Ad #1
- Healing/transformation, "last resort" buyer → Story Ad #2
- Missing ingredient / presell angle → Story Ad #3
- Business model / methodology positioning → Story Ad #4
- Personal development, subconscious, skeptical buyer → Story Ad #5
- Business pivot or pattern interrupt → Story Ad #6

**When the market holds a dominant false belief (medication, diets, generic solutions)?**
→ Use **NHB Ad 1 (Belief Shifter)** or **NHB Ad 4 (Why/Root Cause/Outcome)**

**When the buyer is in a self-blame loop?**
→ Use **NHB Ad 6 (Why/What's Wrong With Me)** or **NHB Ad 5 (Internal Dialogue/Story)**

**When using fill-in-the-blank starting point:**
→ Template 1 = empathy/pain lead; Template 2 = social proof lead; Template 3 = step-by-step/direct; Template 4 = client story; Template 5 = problem/solution structured (Hornung method)

---

### Rule 2: Run the Belief Engineering Diagnostic

Before writing, identify which of the Six Belief Categories are MOST in need of installation for this specific niche and buyer. Ask:

1. **Outcome Beliefs:** Does this buyer believe the outcome is possible, attainable, and worth pursuing? If not — open with outcome-focused headturner. Use the three-part substructure: Possibility → Attainability → Desirability.
2. **Identity/Self Beliefs:** Does this buyer believe they are capable and deserving of the result? If not — use "It's Not Your Fault," absolution of past failure, and "people like me" stories.
3. **Problem Beliefs:** Does this buyer correctly name their own problem? If not — use the Fatal Flaw technique; reframe symptoms as expressions of the real underlying problem.
4. **Solution Beliefs:** Does this buyer believe the mechanism is valid? If not — use root cause + unique mechanism reveal; counter-intuitive insight.
5. **Product Beliefs:** Does this buyer believe this specific offer is the best embodiment? If not — use bonus stack, proof block, and specific differentiators.
6. **Credibility Beliefs:** Does this buyer trust this specific person/brand? If not — use named client count, credential, discovery story, social proof with names/specifics.

The ad must address ALL six, but weight the most deficient category most heavily.

---

### Rule 3: Open to Create Receptive State, Not to Sell

The headturner / opener's ONLY job is to move the prospect from Closed State to Receptive State. It is NOT yet installing beliefs or making the offer. Select an opener type:

**Why PROBLEM opener** (go-to #1):
→ "Why are [target audience] struggling to [goal], even after [common methods tried]?"

**HOW TO OUTCOME opener** (go-to #2):
→ "Here's how I [achieve desirable outcome] in just [small time investment] (without [common pain point]):"

**Biggest Misconception opener** (go-to #3):
→ "What's the biggest misconception about [X]? That [false belief]..."

**Internal Dialogue opener** (for shame/self-blame buyers):
→ "[Emotional quote from prospect's inner voice] 😔 / 🥺"
→ "👆This is a common theme I hear all the time from clients."

**Pattern Interrupt opener** (for pattern interrupt / business model ads):
→ "I hate [COMMON SOLUTION] 👎 [REASON]... So I figured out a way to [OUTCOME] using this dead simple [MECHANISM] 👇 👉LINK"

**If/Then conditional opener** (for niche pain with multiple symptoms):
→ "If [Wrong Action] is [Result clients want]…🙅‍♂️ / If [Common Obstacle] keeps coming up…😕 / If [Exhausting Activity] is causing you to [Negative Emotional Response]…🥲"

---

### Rule 4: Address Belief Categories in the Body

Use these body-copy ingredients in order (adapt sequence to framework type):
1. Root cause / underlying problem (Problem Beliefs)
2. Why mainstream solutions fail — "mask the symptoms" logic (Problem + Solution Beliefs)
3. Counter-intuitive insight if available (Credibility + Solution Beliefs)
4. Unique mechanism reveal (Solution Beliefs)
5. Transformation outcomes list (Outcome Beliefs)
6. "People like me" proof — stories, client types, named results (Identity/Self Beliefs)
7. Offer intro + what it is (Product Beliefs)
8. Bonus stack with personal favorite (Product Beliefs)
9. Named testimonials with specifics (Credibility Beliefs)
10. Sign-off → P.S. counter-objection → P.P.S. guarantee (Credibility Beliefs)

---

### Rule 5: Preserve Exact Voice — Do Not Upgrade or Sanitize

The voice across all sources is **casual authority** — real results stated plainly, deliberately understated, conversational, warm, personally signed.

**ALWAYS preserve:**
- Colloquial phrases: "dead simple," "so damn good," "a good chunk of change," "toggling dials," "just plain silly," "freebie seekers," "pre-sold buyers"
- Emoji as functional punctuation — use exactly as shown: 👉🏼 👇🏼 😋 🫶🏼 💡 🙅‍♂️ 😕 🥲 👎 🫰 🧠 1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣ ✋
- Sign-off by first name only (not "Best regards" or "Sincerely")
- P.S. / P.P.S. structure
- Deliberate grammatical informality: "... …" three-dot ellipsis for dramatic pause; sentence fragments for punch
- The "Are you next?" micro-CTA after testimonials
- "Which is why the bonuses are just plain silly..."
- "My favorite is [X] because [result]"
- "Luckily, it doesn't have to be this way."

---

### Rule 6: NEVER Fabricate These Elements Unless User-Provided

The following categories must NEVER be invented, estimated, rounded up, or implied:

- **Client counts / buyer numbers** — Never write "helped over 10,000 clients" unless the user provides this number
- **Dollar figures / revenue results** — Never write "$35K," "$779," "$1,300 in sales" unless user provided
- **Specific testimonial names** — Never invent names like "Sarah from Ohio" or "Mark, 42"
- **Credentials / certifications** — Never write "board-certified" or "TedX speaker" unless user provided
- **Deadlines** — Never write "Ends Sunday" or "Price goes up in 48 hours" unless genuine deadline exists
- **Proof percentages** — Never write "CPAs drop by 20–25%" unless user provides this
- **Named results from named people** — Anna, Marty, Debbie Belmessieri, etc. are source-specific examples; do not transplant them into user-generated ads as if they are the user's clients

**When proof is not provided by the user:** Use placeholder language: "[Your client count]", "[Specific result your client achieved]", "[Client name] said:", "[Your dollar result]" — and flag to the user that these must be filled in with real data before publishing.

---

### Rule 7: Banned Phrases and Patterns

The following phrases signal generic AI-generated copy and must NEVER appear in generated ads:

**Banned openers / framing language:**
- "Introducing [product name]..." — Never open with "Introducing"
- "Let me ask you something..." / "Let me explain..." — Do not use "Let me" as an opener
- "Are you struggling with [problem]?" — Too generic; use a specific opener type from the swipe files
- "Have you ever wondered..." — Weak curiosity framing; use specific misconception or Why-Problem opener

**Banned hype / marketing-speak:**
- "revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "groundbreaking" / "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" — These are empty amplifiers
- "proven system" / "proven method" without proof backing — Say "proven" only when the proof is provided
- "Transform your life" without specificity — Always name the specific transformation
- "Limited time offer" / "Act now" without a genuine deadline
- "Don't miss out" as a standalone urgency line

**Banned AI-slop structural patterns:**
- Em-dash overuse — Use the three-dot ellipsis "..." as shown in source examples, not — em-dash sequences every sentence
- Bullet lists with headers (Bold: description) as body copy — This reads like a document, not an ad; use paragraph-level prose with emoji bullets
- Opening with "In today's world..." or "In a world where..." 
- Closing with "Take the first step toward..." as a standalone line — use the source's specific CTA patterns
- "Tired of [X]? Imagine [Y]." — This is the most common AI-generated ad pattern; it is not present in any source and must not appear in generated ads

**Banned structural mistakes:**
- Product features before Problem and Solution beliefs are established (skip to Brick 5 error)
- Offer price before the value case is made (exception: Direct Ad #4 "PRICE = WHAT THEY GET" format, which front-loads price as the hook — only use this format deliberately)
- Testimonials with no name, no specific result, no location — these are weak; either provide real ones or flag as placeholder

### Rule 9: Emoji Usage Rules

Emojis in this methodology are functional, not decorative. Follow these specific usage rules:

- **👉 / 👉🏼 / 👉🏻** — Always placed directly before a link. No space between emoji and link. Used multiple times per ad at every link.
- **👇 / 👇🏼** — Placed at the end of the penultimate CTA line, pointing to the final link on the next line.
- **👆** — Placed after a client dialogue quote, before "This is a common theme I hear from clients."
- **💡** — Used after the word "Insight" as a single-word reveal moment. Not used elsewhere.
- **🙅‍♂️ 😕 🥲** — Used as a three-part escalating emotional sequence in the If/Then conditional opener (Headturner #6). Each on its own line after the conditional statement.
- **😋 😃 📈** — Used in Instant Results bullet sections (Direct Ads). One per bullet.
- **1️⃣ 2️⃣ 3️⃣ 4️⃣ 5️⃣** — Used for numbered step sections in step-by-step Direct Ads. One per step.
- **👎** — Used immediately after naming a disliked solution. Appears on the same line.
- **🫶🏼** — Used as a warmth/community close, sparingly.
- **🫰** — Used to signal instant/effortless result in a testimonial ("just like 🫰 got his first 3 sales").
- **🧠** — Used after a vivid problem statement to signal the subconscious/mental angle. Sparingly.
- **✋** — Used before "Let me give you stark warning:" as a pattern interrupt. Sparingly.
- **DO NOT** scatter emojis randomly. DO NOT use emojis as decoration between sentences. DO NOT use emojis not present in the source vocabulary.

### Rule 10: Final Quality Check Before Output

Before delivering a generated ad, verify:

1. **Stage 1 complete:** Does the opener move from Closed to Receptive? Would a cold stranger pause their scroll?
2. **Six Bricks addressed:** Are Outcome, Identity, Problem, Solution, Product, and Credibility beliefs addressed? (Check the weakest ones for this specific niche.)
3. **No fabricated proof:** Are all numbers, names, and specific results either user-provided or clearly flagged as placeholders?
4. **Voice intact:** Is the language casual, direct, and signed by name? Are colloquialisms preserved? Are emojis used functionally?
5. **No banned phrases:** Did "Introducing," "revolutionary," "tired of X, imagine Y," or em-dash overuse sneak in?
6. **Links correctly formatted:** Is 👉 before every link? Is 👇 before the final link?
7. **P.S. present:** Does the ad end with a P.S. counter-objection and P.P.S. guarantee (or at minimum a single-line guarantee)?
8. **Framework minimum requirements met:** Does the ad include all required structural blocks for its type?
